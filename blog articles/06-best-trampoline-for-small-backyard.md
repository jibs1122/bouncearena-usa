# Best Trampoline for Small Backyard in 2026

Small backyards have specific trampoline requirements. Shape matters more than usual, since a 6x9ft oval can fit in a yard where a 10ft round cannot.

The picks below cover footprints from 6ft up to 12ft, with a mix of premium springless models and value direct-to-consumer options. Most also account for the recommended 3 feet of clearance on all sides, which is the difference between a trampoline that fits and one that doesn't.

Here are the ten best trampolines for small backyards in 2026.

## 1. Springfree Compact Round 8ft

The Compact Round is the smallest standard model in the [Springfree](https://www.bouncearenareviews.com/brands/springfree/) lineup and the premium pick for genuinely small yards. Composite rod system under the mat, 175 lb single-user limit, 10-year warranty across all parts.

If your usable space is under 14ft on all sides, this is the natural starting point. Around $1,249.

## 2. Acon Air 10ft Round with Standard Net

[Acon's](https://www.bouncearenareviews.com/brands/acon/) smallest round model and the best bounce quality you can get in a 10ft footprint. 64 coil springs at 7 inches, ASTM certified, galvanized and powder-coated frame.

A solid alternative if you want premium spring bounce rather than the Springfree composite rod feel. Around $899.

## 3. Springfree Compact Oval 6x9ft

The Compact Oval is built for narrow yards. The 6ft width fits between a fence and a garden bed where a round trampoline cannot, while the 9ft length still gives enough surface area for one or two kids.

Same composite rod system and 10-year warranty as the rest of the Springfree range. Around $999.

## 4. Jumpflex FLEX 8ft

The FLEX 8ft is the entry size in the [Jumpflex](https://www.bouncearenareviews.com/brands/jumpflex/) range and the best direct-to-consumer value at this footprint. ASTM certified, galvanized steel frame, 48 coil springs at 5.5 inches.

330 lb combined weight rating. A practical pick if budget is the main constraint. Around $499.

## 5. AlleyOOP 12' Trampoline with Enclosure

The 12ft is the smallest model in the [AlleyOOP](https://www.bouncearenareviews.com/brands/alleyoop/) range and the premium spring pick if a 10ft trampoline feels too small for the family. Made by JumpSport, sold through specialty dealers.

Triple-Fail-Safe enclosure system, lifetime frame warranty. Around $1,274.

## 6. Springfree Medium Round 10ft

The 10ft Medium Round is the most popular size in the Springfree range and the right pick if your yard fits a standard 10ft footprint with clearance. Same springless rod design and warranty as the rest of the lineup.

220 lb single-user limit, suitable for kids through to teens. Around $1,649.

## 7. Jumpflex HERO 10ft

The HERO 10ft is the premium step up from the FLEX 10ft in the Jumpflex range. 64 springs at 7.1 inches, 350 lb single-user rating, 10-year frame warranty.

Best fit if you want Jumpflex's higher-end build without going to a 12ft size. Around $749.

## 8. Acon Air 12ft Round with Standard Net

The 12ft is the smallest "family-sized" Acon and the right pick if your yard fits 12ft with clearance. Same Acon Air spring system as the 14ft and 15ft models, scaled down.

ASTM certified, 35 inch mat-to-ground height. Around $1,049.

## 9. Avyna Pro-Line 12ft Round Above-Ground

[Avyna's](https://www.bouncearenareviews.com/brands/avyna/) smallest round above-ground model and a Dutch-engineered alternative to Springfree at the 12ft size. 80 coil springs, 11-gauge heavy steel frame, lifetime warranty on the frame.

Premium European pricing reflects the build. Around $2,120.

## 10. Vuly Ultra 2 L

The L is the smaller of the two [Vuly](https://www.bouncearenareviews.com/brands/vuly/) Ultra 2 sizes and the most affordable Vuly entry point. Coil spring rather than the Leaf Spring system used on the Thunder 2.

A solid pick if you want Vuly build quality at the low end of their range. From $449.
