# Best Trampoline for Gymnastics in 2026

Gymnastics needs a different kind of trampoline. Rectangles are strongly preferred over rounds because they produce a more uniform bounce and give a longer lift phase per jump.

Most serious gymnasts and tumblers also train without a safety net so they can bail out of a flip cleanly. Frame strength, mat tension, and spring length matter more than the enclosure features that dominate family models.

Here are the ten best trampolines for gymnastics in 2026, from competition-grade installations down to backyard models built around performance bounce.

## 1. MaxAir 14x14 Super Quad Trampoline

[MaxAir](https://www.bouncearenareviews.com/brands/maxair/) is the brand serious gymnasts actually use. Customers include X Games champions, Olympic medalists, American Ninja Warriors, and Hollywood stunt teams.

The 14x14 Super Quad is a competition-grade square trampoline with a Two-String Fly Bed (the same mat construction used in pro gym training facilities). Aspirational pricing reflects that. From around $23,780.

## 2. Akrobat XCITYX 17x12

The XCITYX is [Akrobat's](https://www.bouncearenareviews.com/brands/akrobat/) sport and professional range, designed for advanced users rather than family backyard use. 120 springs at 10.2 inches, 331 lb single-user rating, 12-year spring warranty.

Slovenian engineering with a 70% rebound efficiency claim. Pricing on request from the brand.

## 3. Acon HD 16 Rectangular (10x17ft)

[Acon's](https://www.bouncearenareviews.com/brands/acon/) performance rectangle and the practical premium pick for a serious home gymnastics setup. 140 coil springs, 2 inch galvanized frame, ASTM certified.

The 10x17ft footprint suits tumbling lines and front-to-back flipping practice. Around $2,499.

## 4. Texas Trampolines 9x15 Texas Standard Rectangular

[Texas Trampolines](https://www.bouncearenareviews.com/brands/texas-trampolines/) has been hand-building rectangle and square trampolines in Texas since 1986. The 9x15 Texas Standard ships without a safety net by default, which is what most gymnasts actually want.

Heavy galvanized pipe frame, vinyl-covered safety pads, lifetime frame warranty. US-made. Around $1,749.

## 5. AlleyOOP 10x17 Rectangle

[AlleyOOP's](https://www.bouncearenareviews.com/brands/alleyoop/) rectangle is the premium spring competitor in this category. 108 springs, 2.5mm pre-galvanized steel walls, 280 lb single-user limit, 10-year warranty across mat, springs, and net.

Made by JumpSport, sold through specialty backyard dealers. Around $2,099.

## 6. Akrobat Primus Challenger 14x10

The Primus Challenger is the smaller and more affordable Akrobat rectangle. 104 springs at 8.5 inches, 331 lb single-user rating, 12-year spring warranty.

A solid pick if you want Akrobat bounce performance in a 14x10ft footprint rather than the larger XCITYX. Around $3,099.

## 7. Acon X 17ft Rectangular

The X is the top of Acon's range and includes adjustable spring configurations, which lets a gymnast tune the rebound for different training goals.

Around $3,999, with fully loaded configurations approaching $10,000.

## 8. Avyna Pro-Line 10x17 Rectangle Above-Ground

[Avyna's](https://www.bouncearenareviews.com/brands/avyna/) flagship rectangle and the European premium alternative to Acon and Akrobat. 128 springs, 11-gauge heavy steel frame, lifetime frame warranty.

Tested combined weight capacity over 1,300 lb. Around $3,745.

## 9. Beast K9 10x17 Performance Rectangle

[Beast](https://www.bouncearenareviews.com/brands/beast/) markets the K9 specifically for tricks and athletic use. 124 springs at 9.5 inches, no stated weight limit, galvanized steel frame inside and out.

The most affordable 10x17 rectangle from a performance-positioned brand. Around $1,998.

## 10. Texas Trampolines 7.3x12.3 Kids Delight Rectangular

The smallest Texas Trampolines rectangle and the entry pick for younger gymnasts or smaller yards. Same heavy galvanized pipe construction as the 9x15, just scaled down.

US-made, lifetime frame warranty. Around $1,399.
