# Best In-Ground Trampoline in 2026

In-ground trampolines sit flush with the lawn rather than perched on legs, which gives a cleaner backyard look and a lower fall height. Most installations require digging a hole roughly 40 to 48 inches deep, plus a retaining wall to hold back the soil.

Safety nets are often optional on in-ground models since the fall is to ground level rather than 3 feet down. The brands below all specialize in in-ground construction, with European manufacturers dominating the category.

Here are the ten best in-ground trampolines for 2026.

## 1. North 17x12ft Rectangle In-Ground

[North](https://www.bouncearenareviews.com/brands/north/) is a Swedish brand that distributes in the US exclusively through Capital Play. The 17x12ft is the largest in-ground rectangle in the lineup and the premium pick for serious bouncing.

Heavy duty galvanized steel frame, springs that stretch up to twice the length of standard, 10-year frame warranty. Around $3,249.

## 2. Avyna Pro-Line 14ft Round In-Ground

[Avyna](https://www.bouncearenareviews.com/brands/avyna/) is a Dutch brand and one of the few specialists offering both above-ground and in-ground formats in the same model line. The 14ft Round In-Ground is the family-yard sweet spot.

96 coil springs, 14-gauge heavy steel frame, lifetime frame warranty, 10 years on the springs. Around $2,870.

## 3. Akrobat Primus Flat 17x10

[Akrobat's](https://www.bouncearenareviews.com/brands/akrobat/) Primus Flat range sits at 9.8 inches off the ground (essentially flush with most lawns). Slovenian-engineered with the AkroSPRING Pro System and a 30mm safety pad.

12-year spring warranty. Around $3,299.

## 4. North 14ft Round In-Ground

The smaller round in the North lineup and the right pick for yards that don't fit a 17x12ft rectangle. Same Swedish engineering and spring stretch design as the larger model.

10-year frame warranty. Around $2,549.

## 5. Avyna Pro-Line 10x17 Rectangle In-Ground

The largest standard Pro-Line rectangle in the Avyna in-ground range. 11-gauge heavy steel frame above-ground and 14-gauge for the in-ground version.

Around $3,295.

## 6. Capital Play 11x8ft In-Ground Trampoline

[Capital Play](https://www.bouncearenareviews.com/brands/capital-play/) is the UK in-ground specialist that also handles North's US distribution. The 11x8ft uses a bowl-shaped retaining wall design that reduces excavation depth compared to standard in-ground installs.

80 coil springs, 220 lb single-user limit, commercial grade jump mat. Around $2,135.

## 7. North 15x10ft Rectangle In-Ground

The mid-size rectangle in the North range. 100 coil springs, heavy duty 50mm galvanized steel frame, 2mm wall thickness.

A solid pick if the 17x12ft is too large for your yard. Around $2,849.

## 8. Akrobat Primus Flat 11x8

The smallest model in the Primus Flat range and the entry pick for buyers who want flush-to-ground bounce in a smaller footprint. 80 springs at 8.5 inches.

287 lb single-user rating, 12-year spring warranty. Around $2,395.

## 9. Avyna Pro-Line 12ft Round In-Ground

The smallest round in the Avyna in-ground range and the pick for tighter lawn footprints. 96 coil springs, 14-gauge heavy steel frame.

Around $2,745.

## 10. Avyna Pro-Line MAX 15x15ft Square In-Ground

The largest model in the Avyna in-ground catalog and the premium square format. The MAX line uses a heavier frame than the standard Pro-Line.

Best fit for buyers building a serious backyard installation around the trampoline. Around $4,195.
