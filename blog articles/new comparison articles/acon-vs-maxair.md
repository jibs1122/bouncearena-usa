# ACON vs MaxAir

ACON vs MaxAir is not a normal backyard-trampoline price comparison.

ACON sits in the premium residential performance tier. MaxAir moves into specialist, custom, and gym-style territory, where the price ceiling is much higher and the buyer is usually chasing a more serious training setup.

Start with budget and intended use. If this is for family bouncing, ACON will usually be the more realistic shortlist. If the trampoline is closer to a training investment, MaxAir belongs in the conversation.

## Full Spec Comparison

Key takeaways

- Single-user weight isn't directly comparable: ACON (no published single-user limit; ~300 lb recommended on most models, no limit on high-end models); MaxAir (250 lb).
- ACON offers round, rectangle; MaxAir covers round, square, rectangle.
- If you want a very large trampoline, ACON goes up to 10×17 ft rectangle; MaxAir tops out at 20 ft rectangle.
- ACON leads on warranty (frame 10 yr, mat 5 yr).
- Installation differs: ACON is mainly above-ground with an in-ground option, while MaxAir is above- or in-ground.
