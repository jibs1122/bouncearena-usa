# ACON vs Texas Trampolines

ACON and Texas Trampolines are both heavy-duty coil brands, but Texas is the more specialized one.

Texas leans on raw capacity and a lifetime frame warranty, with a 400 lb single-jumper rating that suits bigger users, though its warranties on the wearing parts are less spelled out. ACON publishes no single-user limit, but recommends a limit of 300 lb, and documents its mat, spring, and pad warranties more clearly.

Choose Texas Trampolines for the higher weight limit and lifetime frame warranty. Choose ACON if you want a more polished all-round trampoline with clearer warranties on the parts that wear.

## Full Spec Comparison

Key takeaways

- Texas Trampolines rates a single jumper to 400 lb; ACON publishes no hard cap and recommends about 300 lb.
- ACON makes rounds and rectangles; Texas Trampolines makes squares and rectangles only.
- If you want a very large trampoline, ACON goes up to 10×17 ft rectangle; Texas Trampolines tops out at 15×15 ft square.
- Texas Trampolines leads on warranty (frame lifetime).
- Installation differs: ACON is mainly above-ground with an in-ground option, while Texas Trampolines is above-ground only.
