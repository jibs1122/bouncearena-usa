# North vs Akrobat

North and Akrobat are both premium choices for buyers who are thinking beyond a basic round backyard trampoline.

North gives you a mix of above-ground and in-ground options with a strong premium-family angle. Akrobat leans more European performance/in-ground, with its own mat and spring system story.

This comparison is best read through installation type, footprint, and how much performance engineering matters for the people using the trampoline.

## Full Spec Comparison

Key takeaways

- The two brands differ on bounce system — North uses traditional coil springs, while Akrobat uses high-performance coil springs.
- Akrobat claims a higher single-user weight limit — up to 331 lb vs 243 lb.
- North offers round, oval, rectangle; Akrobat covers rectangle.
- If you want a very large trampoline, North goes up to 10×18 ft rectangle; Akrobat tops out at 12×17 ft rectangle.
- Akrobat leads on warranty (frame lifetime, springs 12 yr, net 5 yr).
