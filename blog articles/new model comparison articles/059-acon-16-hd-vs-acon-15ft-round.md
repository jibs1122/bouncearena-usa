# ACON 16 HD vs ACON 15ft Round

Same brand and warranty, so shape is the choice here. The 15ft Air is a round; the 16 HD is a rectangle, and the two bounce differently.

## Quick verdict

Choose the 15ft round for general family use and a central spot in the yard. Choose the 16 HD rectangle if older kids want the even, full-mat bounce that suits flips and tricks.

## What matters most

A round pulls jumpers gently back toward the center, which keeps casual bouncing contained and works well for younger kids. A rectangle spreads the bounce evenly across the mat, so you can use the whole surface and get consistent lift, which is what people doing flips or tricks want. Price tracks the shape: the 15ft round is $1,399 to $1,599, the 16 HD rectangle about $2,499. Both share a 10-year frame, 5 years on the mat and springs, and 2 on the pads, and ACON recommends a limit of 300 lb. Pick the shape that matches how the trampoline will be used.
